Travel & Packing Tips

Smart packing makes every trip easier. Keep these tips in mind before you set out, and don’t forget, you can print this full packing checklist here for easy reference while you pack.

  • Pack efficiently: Bring 5–7 outfits that mix and match (neutral colors work best). Include 2 pairs of shoes (one walking, one nicer) and 1 lightweight jacket. Leave space in your suitcase for souvenirs.
  • Keep important documents: Store your passport, tickets, ID, and travel insurance in a waterproof travel wallet. Carry 2 photocopies of each, one in your luggage and one with a trusted contact.
  • Carry a small first-aid kit: Pack 5–10 band-aids, antiseptic wipes, a travel-sized hand sanitizer, and at least 10 doses of ibuprofen or acetaminophen. Keep prescriptions labeled and in your carry-on.
  • Use layers: Weather can change quickly. Bring 2–3 lightweight shirts, 2 sweaters or pullovers, and 1 all-weather jacket. Add or remove layers as needed.
  • Don’t forget chargers: Bring 1 phone charger, 1 travel adapter, and 1 power bank (10,000 mAh or more). For multiple devices, add a small non-surge power strip. Keep chargers in your carry-on for layovers.

Cruise Packing & Travel Tips

Smart packing makes every voyage smoother. Keep these cruise-ready tips in mind before you set sail. And don’t forget, you can print this full packing checklist here for quick reference as you get ready for your trip.

  • Pack efficiently: Bring 5–7 daytime outfits for excursions and sea days, plus 2–3 evening outfits for dinners or formal nights. Add 2 swimsuits, 1–2 cover-ups, and 2 pairs of shoes (sandals and walking shoes). Leave a little space for souvenirs.
  • Bring prescriptions and medicines: Small ships may have limited medical staff. Carry 10–15 doses of aspirin or ibuprofen, 5–10 doses of Dramamine or motion-sickness patches, and 5–10 tablets of Imodium or similar remedies. Label bottles and carry copies of prescriptions.
  • Reserve early: Book shore excursions, spa visits, and specialty dining before you sail or right after boarding. Popular activities fill quickly, so booking ahead guarantees your spot.
  • Choose late dinner seating: If multiple times are offered, choose the later seating. It gives you more flexibility after excursions, allows a relaxed pace, and avoids being rushed.
  • Wear proper footwear: Pack 1 pair of broken-in walking shoes for excursions and 1 pair of sandals for casual wear. Break in shoes at home to avoid blisters that could slow you down.

Ultimate Travel & Cruise Packing List

Packing smart can make or break your trip, and this list goes beyond the basics. Each item includes little notes on why it matters or how it can help you while traveling, so you’ll know exactly why it deserves a spot in your suitcase. You can also print the full packing checklist here for easy reference while you pack. And don’t miss the end of the list, where we’ve added cruise-specific must-haves to make your voyage even smoother.

Documents & Essentials

  • Passport, ID, boarding passes, travel documents (Without these, you literally can’t go.)
  • Credit cards, local currency, and a secure wallet (You need money/payment access once you’re traveling.)
  • Travel insurance paperwork & copies of reservations (Critical if something goes wrong, delays, illness, lost luggage.)
  • Emergency contacts & photocopies of important documents (Backups in case your originals are lost or stolen.)
  • Luggage tags & locks (Helpful for organization & security, but not as critical as the above.)

Clothing

  • Comfortable walking shoes (broken in) (Blisters or sore feet can ruin a trip fast.)
  • Casual outfits for sightseeing shorts, tees, etc. (Your daily go-to clothing.)
  • Sleepwear and undergarments (Absolute basics, you’ll need these every day.)
  • Extra socks and underclothes (Backups keep you comfortable and fresh on longer trips.)
  • Lightweight raincoat or poncho (Weather protection is a must, especially when traveling.)
  • Light sweater/jacket (For chilly evenings, plane rides, or breezy decks.)
  • Hats, sunglasses & beachwear (Essential if you’re heading somewhere sunny or coastal.)
  • Dressy outfit for evenings or special dinners (Important if your itinerary includes formal events, nice dinners.)
  • Swimsuit and cover-up for poolside lounging (Handy for cruises or resorts, but not essential everywhere.)
  • Workout clothes (if using a fitness center, only pack if you know you’ll be using them)

Toiletries & Health

  • Personal prescriptions (with labels) & common medicines (ibuprofen, Imodium, aspirin) (Essential, you can’t always replace these easily while traveling.)
  • Toothbrush, toothpaste, floss (Daily hygiene basics, you don’t want to skip these.)
  • Deodorant, razor, hairbrush/comb (Keeps you fresh and comfortable during your trip.)
  • Shampoo, conditioner, soap/body wash (Important, though hotels/cruise ships sometimes provide basics.)
  • Hand sanitizer, sanitizing wipes & face masks (For hygiene on the go, especially in crowded areas or while traveling.)
  • Small first-aid kit (band-aids, antiseptic wipes) (Handy for blisters, scrapes, or minor issues.)
  • Sunscreen & lip balm with SPF (Critical if you’re going somewhere sunny, helps prevent burns and discomfort.)
  • Aloe vera/after-sun lotion (Useful but only if you get sunburned, good backup, not a must-have for everyone.)

Tech & Accessories

  • Phone & charger (Your lifeline for communication, maps, tickets, and photos #1 essential.)
  • Travel adapter, power bank & power strip (Power access is crucial when abroad or on a cruise, keeps everything running.)
  • Lightweight backpack or day bag (Makes excursions, city walks, and day trips much easier.)
  • Headphones/earbuds (Great for flights, downtime, or noisy environments, quality of life essential.)
  • Camera or GoPro for capturing memories (Important if you want higher-quality photos than your phone provides.)
  • E-reader, tablet, or books for downtime (Nice to have for entertainment, especially on cruises or long flights.)
  • Binoculars (Only essential for certain trips, like Alaska or wildlife-heavy destinations.)

Extras

  • Reusable water bottle (Hydration is key while traveling, saves money, and is eco-friendly.)
  • Guidebook or downloaded maps (Essential for navigation, especially if Wi-Fi/data is unreliable.)
  • Snacks for the trip (Great for long flights, layovers, or excursions where food isn’t handy.)
  • Travel pillow & eye mask (Not essential but makes flights and overnight travel much more comfortable.)
  • Notebook/journal & pen (Nice for notes, memories, or forms, but not a necessity for everyone.)

Cruise-Specific Extras

  • Cruise tickets & shore excursion tickets (Without these, you won’t be boarding or getting into activities.)
  • Seasickness remedies (Dramamine, wristbands, patches) (Essential for comfort, seasickness can ruin your trip fast.)
  • Travel-sized toiletries (ships may have limited selections. Important daily basics that might not be available onboard.)
  • Comfortable sandals (You’ll live in these around the ship and during casual excursions.)
  • Evening wear (smart casual or formal, depending on cruise line dress code. Most cruises require dress-up nights, you can’t skip this.)
  • Small day bag for excursions (Crucial for carrying essentials like sunscreen, water, and tickets when ashore.)
  • Waterproof pouch for cellphones and other devices (Very useful for beach, boat, or rainy port days.)
  • Travel adapter & power strip (non-surge protected, cruise ships forbid surge protectors)
  • Lanyard or badge holder for cruise keycard (Makes life easier, you’ll use your card constantly onboard.)
  • Magnetic hooks (cruise cabin walls are often metal, great for extra storage!)
  • Snorkel gear or water shoes (Optional, good if you prefer your own gear, but most excursions provide rentals.)
